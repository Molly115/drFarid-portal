Part 01

I. The Salaried Employee (Employee Status)

The salaried status is defined by the existence of a Labor Contract. This status is not just about getting paid; it is defined by a legal "Subordination Link."

The Triple Criteria of Subordination

  • The Power to Give Orders: The employer decides what work is done and how.
  • The Power to Monitor: The employer supervises the execution of the work in real-time.
  • The Power to Sanction: The employer can punish or fire the employee for professional faults.

Social & Legal Protections

  • Unemployment Insurance: Access to state funds if let go.
  • Paid Leave: Minimum legal requirements for vacation (e.g., 5 weeks in many EU jurisdictions).
  • Retirement: Mandatory employer contributions to the pension system.
Doctor Farid's Key Insight: "In Digital Law, the subordination link is often challenged by remote work. Does an employer still have 'power to monitor' if you are working from a beach? Yes, through digital tracking and KPI management."
Part 02

II. The Self-Employed & Freelance Status

Self-employment is based on Economic Independence. There is no subordination link; instead, there is a "Commercial Relationship" governed by a Service Contract.

Legal Characteristics

  • Asset Ownership: The freelancer owns their tools (Laptop, Software licenses, Office).
  • Risk Management: If the client doesn't pay, the freelancer bears the financial loss.
  • Fiscal Autonomy: The individual is responsible for their own VAT, Income Tax, and Social contributions.

Regulatory Frameworks

  • Commercial Code: Governs the business transactions.
  • No Labor Law: Freelancers are not protected by the "Labor Code" (No minimum wage, no paid leave).
  • Intellectual Property: Contracts must explicitly state who owns the code or design produced.

Part 04

IV. Workplace Evolution & The "Gig Economy"

The rise of platforms (Uber, Upwork, Deliveroo) has created a "Third Way." This is a major topic in Digital Law today.

The Risk of "False Self-Employment"

Many companies hire "freelancers" but treat them like employees to avoid paying taxes. Digital Law now uses Reclassification. If a court finds a subordination link, the company must pay years of back-taxes and benefits.

Case Study: Social Network Law

How does law govern influencers? Are they employees of the platform (Instagram/TikTok)? No, they are considered independent contractors, but new regulations (like the French Influencer Law) are creating specific "Professional Activities" rules for them.
