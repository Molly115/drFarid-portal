Module 04: Legal Compliance & Production Files

The "Invisible Shield": Securing your International Project through Rigorous Law and Documentation

I. The Three Pillars of Creative Law

When you design a campaign for an international SME, you are handling intellectual property. Ignorance of the law is never an excuse.

1. Copyright (Droit d'Auteur)

Every image, song, and font you use must be licensed. Using a "random Google image" can result in a copyright strike or fine.

2. Image Rights (Droit Ã  l'Image)

If a human face appears in your video or poster, you MUST have a signed authorization form, especially in the EU (GDPR compliance).

3. Terms of Use (CGU)

The showcase site from Module 02 must have a "Legal Mentions" page defining who is responsible for the data collected.

II. Professional Production Files

In a professional agency, "production" starts with a file, not a camera. This file proves to the client (SME) that the project is planned and authorized.

1. The Script & Note of Intent

The Note of Intent explains the "Creative Soul" of the video or poster. It justifies why you chose certain colors, actors, or settings for the international target.

2. Essential Documents List

  • Work Plan: A minute-by-minute schedule of the filming or design process.
  • Filming Authorization: Legal permission to record in specific public or private locations.
  • Talent Release Forms: Contracts signed by models/actors giving permission to use their image globally.
  • Storyboard: The visual translation of the script to prevent wasted time on set.

III. Global Regulatory Standards

Because your audience is international, you must respect the strictest laws (usually GDPR in Europe) to ensure the site is safe worldwide.

Dr. Farid's Legal Insight: "Law is not a barrier to creativity; it is a framework for safety. A professional communicator knows that a project is only as strong as its weakest contract. Always document your licenses!"

Key Focus: The GDPR (RGPD)

If your showcase site has a contact form, you must tell users:

  • What data is being collected.
  • How long it is stored.
  • Who has access to it.

IV. Organizing the Archive

At the end of this module, students must deliver a "Production Bible." This is a single PDF or folder containing:

  1. All signed legal authorizations.
  2. The full technical script.
  3. The budget breakdown for the creative assets.
  4. The note of intent for the international strategy.

This folder is what makes the difference between an amateur "student project" and a professional "agency delivery."
