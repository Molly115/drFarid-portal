02: Formation & Validity of Contracts

The Comprehensive Guide to Legal Agreements in Professional Activity

I. The Pillars of Contractual Validity

Under civil and commercial law, a contract is not just a piece of paper; it is a "meeting of minds." For this meeting to be recognized by a judge, four cumulative conditions must be met:

1. Consent (Le Consentement)

Consent must exist and be free from "vices" (Vices du consentement). There are three main flaws that can cancel a contract:

  • Error: A mistake regarding the essential qualities of the service or person.
  • Fraud (Dol): Intentional deception or lies used to pull the other party into the agreement.
  • Violence/Duress: Physical or moral pressure (including economic dependency) used to force a signature.
2. Legal Capacity (La Capacité)

Not everyone can sign a contract. Parties must be "Capables." This excludes non-emancipated minors and adults under legal guardianship. In business, one must verify the Power of Attorney (does this manager actually have the right to commit the company's money?).

II. Contractual Content & Exploitation

A contract must have a "Content" that is both Licit (legal) and Determined. You cannot sign a contract for an illegal service, nor can you sign for a service that is too vague to be measured.

Digital Validity

The "Click-Wrap" agreement. Law recognizes that clicking "I agree" is a formal expression of consent. However, the employer/provider must ensure the terms were accessible before the click.

Contract Exploitation

This refers to how a contract is used in daily business. Clauses like Confidentiality, Non-compete, and Intellectual Property Transfer are the most exploited in digital marketing.
