Module 03: Communication Campaign Strategy

Designing the Roadmap: Multi-Channel Execution for International Markets

I. The Integrated Communication Plan (ICP)

A campaign is not a single post; it is a synchronized series of messages. For SMEs with international ambitions, the ICP must balance **Global Consistency** with **Local Relevance**.

1. Defining Campaign Objectives

We use the AIDA Model to determine where our target audience is in their journey:

  • Attention: Making the international market aware of the new SME service.
  • Interest: Highlighting unique features (the Value Prop from Module 01).
  • Desire: Creating an emotional connection through storytelling.
  • Action: Driving them to the showcase site (built in Module 02) to convert.

II. Channel Selection: Where to Play?

An international audience consumes media differently depending on their region and professional sector.

Channel Type Platform Example International Role
Owned Media Showcase Website / Blog The "Home Base" where legal and detailed info lives.
Paid Media Social Ads (Meta, LinkedIn) Reaching specific geographic segments (e.g., targeting tourists in the UK).
Earned Media PR / Influencers Building trust through third-party validation in local languages.
Shared Media Social Networks Engagement, community building, and "Viral" potential.
Dr. Farid's Strategic Insight: "In the tourism sector, visual channels like Instagram or Pinterest are for 'The Dream,' while LinkedIn or Email is for 'The Trust.' Never use the same message for both."

III. Social Media Strategy for SMEs

SMEs cannot be everywhere. We must choose channels based on our **Personas**.

The Content Calendar

Planning 4 weeks of posts in advance. Consistency is more important than frequency for international SEO.

Engagement Rules

How do we respond to international comments? Establishing a "Community Management" guide to handle diverse cultural tones.

Visual Continuity

Ensuring that posters, graphic elements, and videos all share the same "Graphic Charter" across all platforms.

IV. The Communications Roadmap

Executing the campaign requires three distinct phases:

  1. The Teasing Phase (Pre-Launch): Building mystery and collecting early-interest emails.
  2. The Launch Phase: High-intensity posting, ad spending, and PR outreach.
  3. The Maintenance Phase: Long-term content (blogs, testimonials) to keep the SEO rankings high on the CMS site.

Key Deliverable: The Media Mix Chart

Students must produce a table showing exactly which content goes on which channel, at what time, and for which target audience.
