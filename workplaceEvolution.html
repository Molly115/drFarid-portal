04: Workplace Evolution

The Digital Transformation of Professional Activity

I. The Revolution of Teleworking

Teleworking is no longer a "favor" from the boss; it is a structured legal framework (Télétravail). It requires a formal agreement or a company charter.

Legal Requirements

  • Equipment: Employer must provide the tools (VPN, Laptop, ergonomic chair).
  • Cost Support: In many countries, the firm must pay a "Remote Work Allowance" for electricity/internet.
  • Right to Disconnect: The most important modern right. An employee has the legal right NOT to answer emails after 6 PM or on weekends.

Management Risks

  • Isolation: The employer must maintain the "Social Link" to prevent depression.
  • Cybersecurity: The employee becomes responsible for the physical security of the data at their home.

II. Adaptability & The "Learning Organization"

The law now recognizes that Skills expire every 2-3 years in the digital sector. Therefore, "Professional Adaptability" is a shared duty.

Key Concept: Employability

If an employer fails to train an employee on new digital tools (like AI or New Marketing Analytics) and then fires them for being "outdated," the dismissal can be declared Unfair. The employer has a legal duty to keep the employee "employable" for the market.

III. The "Gig Economy" & Platform Law

We are moving from "Jobs" to "Tasks." This creates a legal challenge: Are Uber/Deliveroo riders workers or business owners? The evolution of the workplace is currently trending toward Hybridity—where the law tries to give freelancers "employee-like" social protections (insurance, minimum pay per task).
