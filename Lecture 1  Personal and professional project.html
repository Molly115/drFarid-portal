Lecture 01: Self-Knowledge & Introspection

The Foundation of the Personal & Professional Project: Understanding your inner compass before navigating the outer market.

I. The Reflective Approach (L'approche RÃ©flexive)

The PPP is not a static document; it is a reflective process. This means learning how to look back at your experiences to extract meaning for your future. Instead of just saying "I did this," a reflective student asks: "What did this teach me about my values?"

The "Mirror" Principle: Introspection is the ability to analyze your own thoughts and emotions. In this course, we use it to discover your Curiosity, Aspirations, and Motivations.

1. Defining Aspirations vs. Motivations

  • Aspirations: Your long-term dreams and the "ideal" version of your career.
  • Motivations: The "Engine." What gets you out of bed? Is it money, creative freedom, helping others, or technical mastery?

Savoirs (Knowledge)

Academic theories, languages, and facts you have learned in your B.U.T. courses.

III. Finding Your "Ikigai"

How do we align introspection with the professional market? We use the Japanese concept of Ikigai (A Reason for Being).

To find your path, you must find the intersection of:

  1. What you Love (Passion).
  2. What you are Good at (Profession).
  3. What the World Needs (Mission).
  4. What you can be Paid for (Vocation).
Introspection Exercise: List three moments in your life where you felt truly "in the flow." For each, identify which Values (Independence, Altruism, Security, Logic) were present.

IV. Taking Ownership of the Course

Self-knowledge isn't just about your personality; it's about Personal Strategy. You must identify which "Skill Blocks" in your B.U.T. specialty align with your natural strengths.

Identifying Skill Blocks:

  • Are you more suited for the Creative/Communication pathway?
  • Or do you prefer the Strategy/Marketing data-driven pathway?

By the end of this module, you should be able to reference your personal skills and associate them with real-life situations (Star Method) to prepare for your future internship interviews.
