Lecture 02: Personal Branding & E-Reputation

The Digital Identity Strategy: Controlling your professional image and setting up a digital watch system.

I. Understanding the E-Reputation Cycle

E-Reputation is the perception that internet users have of you, based on the traces you leave behind. It is a mix of Personal Identity (who you say you are) and Digital Footprint (what others find when they search for you).

1. The Issues & Risks

In the digital age, your reputation is "permanent, searchable, and replicable." One unprofessional post from five years ago can disrupt a career goal today.

The "Google Test": 70% of recruiters admit to rejecting candidates based on their social media content. If you aren't managing your image, Google's algorithm is managing it for you.

II. Key Steps in Personal Branding

Personal Branding is the act of treating yourself as a brand. It is about differentiation and consistency.

1. Defining the Core

What is your unique value proposition? What "skill block" do you want to be known for (e.g., The Creative Designer or The Data Strategist)?

2. Visual Identity

Using consistent professional headshots, color palettes, and typography across your LinkedIn, E-Portfolio, and CV.

3. Content Strategy

Sharing your expertise. It‚Äôs not just about "looking for a job," but about showing you are an active member of your professional community.

III. Setting up a Digital Watch

To control your image, you must monitor it. A "Digital Watch" (Veille Digitale) is an automated system that alerts you whenever your name or interest areas appear online.

The 3-Step Monitoring Setup:

1
Passive Monitoring: Use Google Alerts for your name and variations of it. This tells you immediately if you are being mentioned.
2
Active Monitoring: Using professional social networks (LinkedIn) to monitor industry trends and specific company catchment areas.
3
Privacy Audit: Regularly checking your social media privacy settings and removing "noise" (unprofessional tags or photos).

IV. Mastering Professional Social Networks

Your LinkedIn profile is more than a CV; it is your Digital Identity Hub.

  • Headline Optimization: Use keywords that match the ROME files (professional standards) of your target job.
  • Endorsements & Recommendations: Social proof that your "Savoir-faire" is real.
  • Network Development: Building a "Network Card" by connecting with professionals you met during visits to Fab Labs or Incubators.

By the end of this lecture, students will have a personal strategy to control their "Digital Shadow" and turn it into a Professional Asset.
