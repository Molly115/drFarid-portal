I. Strategic Digital Presence

A "Digital Presence" is not just a website; it is an ecosystem. For an SME or a tourism agency, the website acts as the **Central Hub**, while social networks act as the **Satellites** that drive traffic to the hub.

1. Defining the Showcase Website (Site Vitrine)

Unlike an e-commerce site, a showcase site is designed to **convert interest into contact**. Its primary goals are:

  • Establishing **Authority** and Professionalism.
  • Presenting the **Value Proposition** clearly.
  • Providing a clear **Call to Action (CTA)** (e.g., "Request a Quote", "Book a Consultation").

II. CMS Selection & Architecture

Choosing a Content Management System (CMS) is a critical business decision. In this course, we focus on tools that allow for **rapid deployment** and **easy maintenance** by non-technical SME staff.

The CMS Advantage: CMS tools decouple "Content" from "Code." This allows an agency to design the structure once, while the client (SME) can update texts and images without breaking the design.

1. Popular Options for SMEs:

  • WordPress: High flexibility, massive plugin ecosystem, but requires frequent security updates.
  • Webflow/Wix Studio: High design control, "closed" hosting (more secure), excellent for visual storytelling.
  • Shopify: Only if the "product" involves direct transaction.

2. Information Architecture (Sitemap)

Before touching the CMS, you must build the sitemap. An international site usually follows this hierarchy:

Home > Services/Product > About Us > Case Studies/Gallery > Contact (Multilingual)

III. Designing for an International Target

User Experience (UX) changes across borders. What a French user expects in terms of navigation may differ from an American or Asian user.

Language Switching:

Don't just use flags (flags represent countries, not languages). Use clear text like "English / Français".

Loading Speed:

International users may have different bandwidths. Optimization of images (WebP format) is non-negotiable.

Mobile-First:

In many international markets, the primary device is the smartphone. The site MUST be responsive.

Dr. Farid's Technical Tip: "In the SME world, speed equals trust. A site that takes more than 3 seconds to load on an international server will lose 50% of its potential leads before they even see the logo."

IV. Step-by-Step Development Workflow

To ensure project success, follow this sequence:

  1. Wireframing: Low-fidelity sketches of the layout (UX).
  2. Visual Identity: Applying the "Graphic Elements" (UI) – Colors, Typography, Posters.
  3. CMS Implementation: Building the pages and setting up the navigation.
  4. SEO Basics: Meta titles, descriptions, and Alt-tags for images in both languages.
  5. Quality Assurance (QA): Testing every link and form on multiple browsers.
